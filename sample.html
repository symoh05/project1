The Journey of Barack Obama

Barack Hussein Obama II was born on August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii. He made history as the first African American to be elected president of the United States, serving from January 20, 2009, to January 20, 2017.

Obama's father, Barack Obama Sr., was a Kenyan economist. His mother, Ann Dunham, was an American anthropologist. This multicultural background shaped his worldview and political style.

Obama attended Occidental College, then transferred to Columbia University. After working in Chicago, he earned a law degree from Harvard where he became the first Black president of the Harvard Law Review.

"Change will not come if we wait for some other person or some other time. We are the ones we've been waiting for. We are the change that we seek."

The Obama Family

In 1989, Barack met Michelle Robinson. They married in 1992 and had two daughters:

  • Malia Ann Obama - Born July 4, 1998
  • Sasha Obama - Born June 10, 2001

Sasha became the youngest child to live in the White House since JFK Jr. The family was admired for their closeness and charm throughout their time in office.
